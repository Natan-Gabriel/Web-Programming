Why is new York called Big Apple?

When did New York receive this nickname?

"The Big Apple" is a nickname for New York City. It was first popularized in the 1920s by John J. Fitz Gerald. Its popularity since the 1970s is due in part to a promotional campaign by the New York tourist authorities.

John J. Fitz Gerald was a sports writer for the New York Morning Telegraph.
